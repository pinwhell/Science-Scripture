## 3. Ontological Guardrails

### 3.1 Actuality as a Non-Physical Variable
A successful unification must resist the urge to include "selection" or "collapse" as a dynamical process within the math.

-   **Formalism**: The equations must describe probability distributions and state evolution without specifying which outcome is "instantiated."
-   **Blocked Errors**: This blocks GRW-style modified dynamics, Everettian "many-worlds" inflation (reifying all possibilities as equally actual), and any hidden "chooser" variables.
-   **PCI Insight**: Actuality is not a physical variable; it is the ontological state of the system which remains "outside" the formalism.

### 3.2 The Logos as the Condition of Intelligibility
The intelligibility of a unified theory—the fact that the universe is narratable and law-consistent—is not a derivation from the theory but its prerequisite.

-   **Constraint**: The Logos (the Word, the Ground of Truth) is the condition that makes any theory possible.
-   **Directionality**: The Logos constrains the *metaphysical interpretation* of the theory, not its *mathematical form*. We do not ask the math to prove the Logos; we recognize the Logos as the reason there is math to describe.
-   **Biblical Grounding**: "In Him all things hold together" (Col 1:17). Christ is the sustainer of the structure, not a component within it.
