Participatory Agency in Open Quantum Systems:
A Logos-Grounded Structural Derivation
Abstract This paper presents a metaphysical interpretation of quantum foundations described as a "Participatory Constraint on Indeterminacy" (PCI). We demonstrate that while quantum mechanics allows for structural openness (indeterminacy), the meaning of this openness cannot be grounded in abstract principles without leading to infinite regress or category errors. We posit that valid instantiation requires Personhood (Logos) as the ultimate ground of intelligibility, with finite agencies operating as participatory constraints on this pre-existing intelligibility. This model preserves the conservation laws of physics while reframing the measurement problem through a non-reductive, relationally grounded ontology. Crucially, this framework strictly maintains that physics presupposes the Logos; it does not derive the Logos from physics.

1. Physical Foundations: The Reality of Openness
We begin with the established limits of mechanistic closure.

Quantum Indeterminacy: The evolution $|\psi\rangle \to |\psi(t)\rangle$ is deterministic, but the measurement $\hat{M}$ yields outcomes $k$ with probability $P(k) = |\langle k|\psi\rangle|^2$, without specified cause for individual events.
No Local Hidden Variables: Bell’s Theorem and subsequent experiments (Aspect/Zeilinger) rule out local hidden variables, confirming true indeterminacy or non-locality.
Axiomatic Observation: The physical universe is not causally closed under mechanism. It contains "causal slack"—degrees of freedom that are mathematically real but physically determined only at the moment of actualization.

2. The Logic of Actuality and Truth
A purely structural approach suggests an abstract "Selector" fills this gap. However, Physics explains distribution; Physics does not explain actuality as such. PCI is about what actuality means, not how specific occurrences happen.

Distinction of Randomness: We explicitly distinguish three senses of randomness:

Statistical Randomness (Physics/Distribution)
Causal Randomness (Mechanism/Indeterminacy)
Ontological Contingency (Actuality/Fact) PCI does not deny randomness at the level of statistics or causal explanation. It denies only that "randomness" can serve as an ontological account of actuality itself.
The Theological Correction (Truth Grounding): If the instantiation of a history is non-random in the ontological sense, it implies Intent. Intent implies Personhood. Therefore, the ultimate ground of actualization cannot be a "mechanism" or a "law," but must be the Logos.

Definition of Personhood: Personhood here denotes not a psychological homunculus or inner observer, but the irreducible unity of normativity, intentionality, and intelligibility—features that cannot be coherently grounded in impersonal structure alone.

Axiom (Non-Competitive Explanation): Personal explanations (intent, meaning, purpose) do not compete with physical explanations, because they answer a different kind of question. The Logos grounds the reality of events, not their statistical structure.

Definition of Logos-Grounding: The Logos grounds intelligibility not as an abstract rational structure, but as living Truth in which knowing, being, and meaning are inseparable. Crucially, the Logos is never a variable within a physical equation, but the ground of the equation's possibility. The Logos grounds the actuality of creation as intelligible order, not the selection of particular physical outcomes within that order. Contingency is willed as contingency.

The Logos as Condition: The Logos functions here not as an explanatory posit within a theory, but as the condition under which explanation, theory, and justification are possible at all.

Measurement Problem Disclaimer: PCI leaves every technical problem in quantum foundations exactly where it stands. Its sole contribution is to prevent a category mistake about what a "solution" could possibly mean. It reframes the problem as a question about actuality rather than dynamics.

3. Agency as Participatory Constraint
We redefine finite agency not as an independent source of power, but as a Participatory Constraint.

Definition: Agency does not select outcomes instead of physical law. Agency is the mode by which a law-consistent history is lived as this history. It does not "steer," "bias," or "control" the physics; it inhabits the openness provided by the physics. Agency is narrative, not nomological.

Formal Status of $I(participation)$: To strictly honor the limits of physics and theology:

$I(participation)$ is not a physical modifier of probabilities.
It is a hermeneutic index defined only at the level of narrative description (similar to "fitness" in evolutionary narratives).
It never enters the forward dynamics of the system.
The Probability Statement: For agent-involving systems, we state that the Born distribution remains complete as physics, but insufficient as a total explanation of why this history rather than another is instantiated.

$$ \text{Physics explains Probabilities. Actuality is not a Probability.} $$

The Work of PCI: PCI is not retained because it explains events, but because it explains why explanations themselves are answerable to truth rather than mere description.

Theological Safeguard: The Logos is not causally downstream of participatory constraints; rather, participatory agency is itself eternally sustained within the providential act of Logos, preserving both divine sovereignty and genuine creaturely freedom.

Neural systems operate at the edge of chaos (Criticality).
Microscopic fluctuations (ion channel gating, synaptic vesicle release) are quantum-indeterminate events.
The brain's architecture allows these microscopic indeterminacies to cascade into macroscopic signals.
Scientific Humility: Quantum indeterminacy is merely illustrative, not foundational. It is one source of variability among many (thermal, biochemical, chaotic). Nothing in this model hinges on quantum indeterminacy specifically; contingency is multiscale and already sufficient. The brain is a creaturely instrument, contingent and fragile; it does not disclose the Logos, but discloses contingency.

6. Scientific Falsifiability and Constraints
To meet the Gold Standard of science, we must clearly define the empirical limits of this model.

Prediction 1 (The Null Hypothesis): In systems without agency (dead matter), the system follows Born rules and classical statistics perfectly.

Prediction 2 (The Consistency Constraint): If any physical instantiation of participatory openness exists, it must appear within already-known stochastic regimes. No deviation from standard statistics should be expected.

The Honest Empirical Conclusion: PCI makes no empirical predictions beyond standard physics; it is an interpretive framework for understanding agency, not a theory of physical deviation. No experiment can ever demonstrate participatory agency or Logos. At best, experiments can show whether the physical conditions required for such participation plausibly exist.

Falsifiability Immunity: The existence of the Logos, Intrinsic Meaning, and Personhood are metaphysical commitments, not hypothesis to be tested.
